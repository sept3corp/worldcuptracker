# World Cup 2026 Betting Tracker — Build Spec

## 1. What this is

A web app for a 4-person betting group tracking the FIFA World Cup 2026. Each
person ("owner") drafted 12 World Cup teams and 8 top-scorer candidates before
the tournament. The app needs to:

1. Pull live World Cup 2026 results.
2. Match results against each owner's draft.
3. Calculate who owes what to whom, using the payout rules below.
4. Display a settlement ledger + status tables, refreshed live, viewable as a
   shareable hosted web page (view-only — no login, no backend database
   required beyond whatever the live data source needs).

The four owners are: **Den**, **Jong**, **Kao**, **Pi**.

## 3. Payout rules (locked, fully confirmed with user)

There are **two fundamentally different payout structures** in this bet.
Getting this distinction right is the most important part of the engine:

- **Match-win payouts**: strictly head-to-head between the two owners whose
  teams played each other in that specific match. The losing team's owner
  pays the winning team's owner. The other two owners are not involved at all,
  regardless of who else is in the bet.
- **Bonus payouts** (Champion bonus, goal payouts, Golden Boot bonus): paid
  by **each of the other 3 owners individually** to the relevant owner. E.g.
  a goal payout of ฿80 means the scoring player's owner receives ฿80 from
  owner B, ฿80 from owner C, and ฿80 from owner D — ฿240 total — regardless
  of which team the goal was scored against.

### 3.1 Match-win payouts (head-to-head only)

| Round | Payout to winner's owner | Paid by |
|---|---|---|
| Group stage | ฿150 per win | Losing team's owner |
| Group stage draw | ฿0 | — (no payout either direction) |
| Round of 32 | ฿150 | Losing team's owner |
| Round of 16 | ฿200 | Losing team's owner |
| Quarterfinal | ฿400 | Losing team's owner |
| Semifinal | ฿600 | Losing team's owner |
| Third Place Playoff | ฿600 | Losing team's owner |
| Final | ฿800 | Losing (runner-up) team's owner |

**Knockout matches decided by penalty shootout**: the penalty-shootout
winner is treated as the match winner for payout purposes. The 90-minute
(or extra-time) result does not matter once a shootout occurs — only the
final winner is paid; the loser pays regardless of how close the match was.

### 3.2 Champion bonus (pool-style, on top of the Final match-win payout)

When a team wins the Final, that team's owner receives **both**:
1. The standard Final match-win payout (฿800) from the runner-up's owner only.
2. An additional **Champion bonus of ฿1,200 from EACH of the other 3 owners**
   (฿3,600 total) — this is separate money, not a 3-way split of ฿1,200.

So the champion's owner nets ฿800 + ฿3,600 = ฿4,400 from the Final alone,
on top of everything accumulated in earlier rounds.

### 3.3 Goal payouts (pool-style)

Every goal scored by one of the 31 drafted players triggers a payout from
each of the other 3 owners to the scoring player's owner.

| Stage | Payout per goal | Paid by |
|---|---|---|
| Group stage | ฿80 | Each of the other 3 owners (฿240 total per goal) |
| Any knockout match (Round of 32 through Final, **including the Third Place Playoff**) | ฿100 | Each of the other 3 owners (฿300 total per goal) |

Rules:
- **Own goals never count.** If a drafted player is credited with an own
  goal by official records, it does NOT trigger a payout. Only goals
  officially credited to the player as a "scored goal" count.
- **No cap on goals per match.** A hat-trick in the group stage = 3 × ฿80 =
  ฿240 owed by each of the other 3 owners (฿720 total to the scorer's owner)
  for that single match.
- Goals only count if scored by one of the 31 drafted players specifically.
  Goals by any other player in the tournament are irrelevant to this bet.

### 3.4 Top scorer / Golden Boot bonus (pool-style, resolved once at tournament end)

Whichever owner's drafted player finishes the entire tournament as the
**outright** top scorer (Golden Boot winner) receives **฿800 from each of
the other 3 owners** (฿2,400 total), in addition to all the per-goal payouts
already earned by that player throughout the tournament.

- This only resolves once, at the end of the tournament.
- If the Golden Boot is shared/tied by official FIFA rules between multiple
  players, and more than one of them is one of the 31 drafted players
  owned by different owners — ask the user how to split this rather than
  assuming. This edge case was not covered in the confirmed rules.
- If the actual Golden Boot winner is NOT one of the 31 drafted players at
  all, no one receives this bonus.

## 4. Settlement engine logic (what the app must compute)

For any point in the tournament, the app should be able to produce:

1. **A per-match ledger**: each completed match, which two owners' teams (if
   any from the draft) were involved, the result, and the resulting
   head-to-head payout (who owes whom, how much, which round).
2. **A per-goal ledger**: each goal scored by a drafted player, which match/
   round it was in, and the resulting 3-way payout (owner of scorer receives
   from the other 3).
3. **A running net balance per owner**: total received minus total owed,
   summed across all match-win payouts, goal payouts, and any bonuses
   resolved so far. This requires resolving the *net* between each pair of
   owners (since match-win debts run in both directions over the
   tournament) — show both the gross ledger and the simplified net-per-pair
   summary (e.g. "Jong owes Den ฿350 net" rather than listing every
   individual transaction).
4. **Live/pending bonus tracking**: Champion bonus and Golden Boot bonus
   should show as "pending" / unresolved with a leaderboard of current
   standing (e.g. current top scorer among the 31 drafted players, current
   surviving teams per owner) until the tournament actually ends.
5. **Team status table**: per owner, list of their 12 teams with current
   group standing / knockout round reached / eliminated status.
6. **Scorer status table**: per owner, list of their players with current
   goal tally and cumulative earnings from that player alone.

## 5. Data source

**Primary candidate**: `https://worldcup26.ir` — a free, open-source,
no-API-key REST API built specifically for WC 2026.
- `GET /get/games` — all 104 matches, scores, scorers (fields seen in
  public docs: `home_score`, `away_score`, `home_scorers`, `away_scorers`,
  `group`, `matchday`, `finished`, `type` — note `type` distinguishes group
  vs knockout matches, useful for the goal-payout rate split).
- `GET /get/groups` — group standings.
- `GET /get/teams` — team list/IDs.

**Known risks — handle defensively**:
- This is an unofficial, donation-funded hobby project with no SLA. It may
  go down, change its response format, or get rate-limited without notice.
- The site's `robots.txt` blocks server-side/automated fetching from at
  least some tooling (confirmed: Anthropic's own `web_fetch` tool was
  blocked by this). It is *unknown* whether direct browser-side `fetch()`
  calls (CORS-permitting) from a hosted static page will work — this needs
  to be tested live once hosted, not assumed.
- The `home_scorers`/`away_scorers` fields' reliability for mapping to the
  31 drafted player names is unverified — names may not exactly match the
  shorthand used in the draft (e.g. "Alvarez" vs a full official name like
  "Julián Álvarez"). Build a name-matching/aliasing layer rather than exact
  string matching, and flag unmatched scorer entries for manual review
  rather than silently dropping them.
- User has explicitly said: keep this simple, just attempt the live API,
  no manual-paste fallback UI needed. If the live fetch fails, show a clear
  "data unavailable, try refresh" error state — do not fabricate or cache
  stale data silently.

**Fallback / verification approach if `worldcup26.ir` doesn't pan out**:
search for alternatives (`openfootball/worldcup.json` on GitHub is a
free, no-key, daily-updated JSON dataset as a backup, though not live/real-
time — updated roughly once a day by the maintainer). Confirm with the user
before switching data sources, since switching changes what "live" means.

## 6. Build requirements (confirmed with user)

- **Hosting model**: simple shareable web page, view-only. User updates/
  shares the link with friends; no per-user login or accounts.
- **Refresh behavior**: auto-fetch live data when the page loads, PLUS a
  manual refresh button for re-fetching on demand.
- **Team status detail level**: show group position, W-D-L record, and
  points per team — no need for next-match date/opponent or full mini group
  tables (user explicitly chose the simpler option).
- **Scorer data refresh**: ideally live via the same API's match-scorer
  fields. If that proves unreliable, the documented fallback approach
  discussed with the user was a daily web-search-based snapshot of official
  FIFA top-scorer data — but per the user's later "keep it simple" decision,
  start with attempting live data only; don't build the manual/snapshot
  fallback UI unless the live approach demonstrably fails.
- **No manual data-paste fallback UI** — explicitly declined by user.
- **Visual design**: this is a functional tool/dashboard for checking daily,
  not a branded showcase. Keep it clean, readable, and fast — prioritize
  clarity of the ledger and tables over visual flourish.
- **Timestamp**: show "last updated" time so users know data freshness.

## 7. Open questions / edge cases not yet resolved

Flag these to the user if/when they come up rather than assuming:

1. **Golden Boot tie/shared award** across multiple drafted players owned
   by different owners — no rule confirmed for splitting this.
2. **Exact name matching** between the API's scorer fields and the 31
   drafted player names (shorthand vs. full official names) — will need
   a manual alias map, built and refined empirically once real data is
   flowing.
3. **CORS / live-fetch viability** of `worldcup26.ir` from a browser
   context — unverified, test as the first build step before investing in
   the rest of the UI.
4. **Best 8 third-place teams advancing to Round of 32**: with the new
   48-team format, some drafted teams may advance not as group winners/
   runners-up but as one of the best 8 third-place teams across all 12
   groups. The status table logic must account for this — a 3rd-place
   finish in the group doesn't automatically mean elimination.
